About Our Village

Dayali Bigha is a traditional Indian village located in the Nardiganj Subdivision of Nawada district, Bihar, India. According to the 2011 Census, our village has a population of 759 people residing in 114 households. The village is primarily an agricultural community, where farming is not just a profession but a way of life. The fertile lands surrounding our village produce a variety of crops including rice, wheat, pulses, and vegetables.

The community is known for its warm hospitality, strong cultural values, and close-knit social bonds. With an average household size of approximately 6 people per house, Dayali Bigha represents the heart of India - where ancient traditions meet modern aspirations, creating a unique blend of heritage and progress. Our village falls under the Ichuakarna Gram Panchayat and Nardiganj Block Panchayat.

Village Administration

Dayali Bigha is governed through a structured administrative system that ensures the welfare and development of all residents. Our village falls under the Ichuakarna Gram Panchayat (Gram Panchayat Code: 98354) and the Nardiganj Block Panchayat (Block Code: 2502). The village is identified by Village Code 257488 and is part of the Nardiganj Assembly and Parliament Constituency.
